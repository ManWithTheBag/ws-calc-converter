least common multiple

the least common multiple (LCM) of two integers m and n is the smallest natural number that is divisible by both m and n.

examples
find the HCF of numbers 450 and 390.
let's represent numbers as the product of their prime factors, for it use factoring a number into prime factors
450 = 2 · 3 · 3 · 5 · 5 390 = 2 · 3 · 5 · 13
we see that the common factors are 2, 3 and 5. then HCF(450, 390) = 2 · 3 · 5 = 30.

knowing the HCF of these numbers, you can easily find their LCM. to do this, the product of numbers should be divided by their HCF:
450 · 390 / 30
= 5850
