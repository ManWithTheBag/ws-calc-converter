factoring a number into prime factors

any natural number n > 1 can be represented as a product of prime numbers. this representation is called the factorization of n into prime factors.
algorithm for factoring numbers into prime factors: draw a vertical line. to the left of the line we write the number. to the right of the line we write the prime divisor of this number. on the left we write the number that was formed as a result of division. continue the process until there is 1 left on the left.
examples
factor the number 36
draw a line and write 36 on the left. the smallest prime divisor of 36 is 2. divide
36 / 2
= 18. 18 write it under the number 36.
